Our Vision

We passionately believe to restore the lost humanity & fill the deprived ones with content. Reorienting the world towards being more kind & happier. To make women more independent, help people grasp their passion & to make people believe they are not alone, neither is it a cruel world.

Our Objective

Social Service

"Not all of us can do great things, but we can do small things with great love." -Mother Teresa

With the same objective to do my part towards the society we established the NGO. Our main objective is to do every-single-thing in our power to be able to make this world a better place to live in. We believe if each one of us started helping a single person in our lifetime, we could help millions of underprivileged and help them grow into a better person.

Development

"Every day do something that will inch you closer to a better tomorrow."

No one chooses to be underprivileged. They just need a push to be able to get a life with basic necessities. We aim to be able to be someone's wind beneath their wings. Helping an individual grow and develop, and providing a path to be able to live a healthy and peaceful life is the sole purpose.

Awareness

" Awareness is like the sun. When it shines on things, they are transformed." -Thich Nhat Hanh

Awareness tends to be the key to growing and developing as an individual. An open mind is like a sponge, the more you spill knowledge the more it absorbs. Awareness is empowering. Spreading the awareness and breaking the taboos of the society is what we seek as one of our objectives.

Our Services

Food Drives

As a child, we've always learnt that food is the basic necessity of life. Yet, According to FAO estimates in ‘The State of Food Security and Nutrition in the World, 2020 report, 189.2 million people are undernourished in India. By this measure 14% of the population is undernourished in India.

To ensure that we're able to do everything that is in our power, we organize food stalls every Sunday and on different occasions, visiting and distributing food at orphanages and old age homes has always been close to our hearts.

Cloths Distribution

"My old clothes don't fit me, but will surely fit someone else."

Clothes are the basic necessity of life and should be provided to every single individual. Keeping this in our minds we started an initiative to donate clothes in the old age homes, orphanages and ashrams.

Menstrual Health

70 percent of mothers with menstruating daughters considered menstruation as dirty and 71 percent adolescent girls remained unaware of menstruation till menarche. A 2014 UNICEF report pointed out that in Tamil Nadu, 79 percent girls and women were unaware of menstrual hygiene practices. The percentage was 66% in Uttar Pradesh, 56% in Rajasthan and 51% in West Bengal. Only 2 to 3 per cent women in rural India are estimated to use sanitary napkins. These are some most shocking statistics about menstrual awareness in India. Taking a step towards uprooting and breaking the taboo, we stand up to spread awareness.
